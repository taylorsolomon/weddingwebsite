Food

Ted's Bulletin

Downtown D.C. / Reston VA

Taylor and Emily, like most D.C. area residents, love Ted's Bulletin's homemade pop-tarts and hand-spun milkshakes. If you do not have a sweet tooth, Ted's also has real-people food. If you're looking for an upscale '50s throwback, eat here.

Uncle Julio's

Reston, VA (Close to Hotels)

If you venture the short drive to Uncle Julio’s in Reston Town Center, you might run into members of the Wymer and Solomon families as this is a go-to for both. Get the guacamole.

Clyde's

Ashburn, VA / Reston, VA

Much like Ted’s Bulletin, Clyde’s is a well-known establishment in the D.C. Metro area, with locations in Maryland, downtown D.C., and Northern Virginia. Clyde’s at Willow Creek is close to Dulles and is in an antique heavy timber building. There is also a Clyde’s in Reston Town Center.

Rockland's BBQ

Arlington, VA / DC

When walking into Rocklands, you will be greeted by traditional BBQ and a wide array of hot sauces that challenge even the bravest of Texan palettes. Emily loves the Sweet Potato Fries, but Taylor is a sucker for their fried okra and the Pearl–a layer of pulled chicken, a layer of beans, and a layer of Mac & Cheese, topped with Rockland's homemade BBQ.

The site of Paradise Springs Winery lies on 36 acres nestled in the quiet corner of Clifton, VA on the edge of Fairfax County. The property borders Hemlock Regional Park with the Bull Run River flowing only steps through the woods.

The Village Winery

This winery is in Waterford, Virginia, where Taylor’s family lives. The vineyard is very close to the Solomon family home and is a favorite of Deanna, Taylor’s mom.

Fun Virginia Wine Fact

President Thomas Jefferson was an aspiring viticulturist and had plans to make Monticello a successful vineyard. However, he unfortunately he failed because the grape he was using, the Bland grape, was not suited for Virginia soil. Years later, Dr. Daniel Norborne Norton found a way to realize Thomas Jefferson’s dream by cross-pollinating the grape, thus making the Norton Wine. This wine is distinctly Virginia in its history and can be found at Bull Run Winery.

Flying Dog is well-known in the D.C. metro area for their IPAs. Known for experimentation, this brewery has seasonal "extreme" beers featuring flavors like Sriracha and Old Bay Seasoning.

Ashburn, VA (Close to Hotels)

Lost Rhino comes from a surfing term, rhino chaser; someone out to find the best waves, the biggest waves—an adventurer. Taylor has yet to adventure his way to this brewery, but has never had a beer from here that he did not like.

Catoctin Creek Distillery

Purcellville, VA

Catoctin Creek is owned by Solomon-family friends and produces some of the best whiskey in Virginia.
