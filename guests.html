Paradise Springs Winery

Clifton, VA

The site of Paradise Springs Winery lies on 36 acres nestled in the quiet corner of Clifton, VA on the edge of Fairfax County. The property borders Hemlock Regional Park with the Bull Run River flowing only steps through the woods. Originally, the property was part of a one-thousand acre land grant from Lord Fairfax in 1716 to the family’s direct ancestors and has been passed down through the generations since. Several hundred acres still remain in the family today.

Fun Virginia Wine Fact

President Thomas Jefferson was an aspiring viticulturist and had plans to make Monticello a successful vineyard. However, he unfortunately he failed because the grape he was using, the Bland grape, was not suited for Virginia soil. Years late, Dr. Daniel Norborne Norton found a way to realize Thomas Jefferson’s dream by cross-pollinating the grape, thus making the Norton Wine. This wine is distinctly Virginia in its history and can be found at Bull Run Winery.

Lost Rhino Brewery

Ashburn, VA (Close to Hotels)

Lost Rhino comes from a surfing term, rhino chaser; someone out to find the best waves, the biggest waves—an adventurer. Before opening the doors to their new brewery, Matt Hagerman and Favio Garcia set out on an adventure of their own. Travelling across America in search of the best quality beers, biggest surprises, and good company. Not quite mad scientists, but certainly a pair of bold, creative minds out to challenge themselves. A pair of lost rhinos. They decided to bring the spirit of West Coast brewing to the mid-atlantic. Our creativity is inspired by the experiences brought on by choosing the scenic route, the tough challenge, the road less traveled. We brew to bring beer drinkers something interesting, revered traditions, and great local beers. Everything we craft is personal, from cultivating yeast in-house to supporting our community and Virginia farmers.
