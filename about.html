About The Couple

About Taylor

Taylor was born outside of Dallas, Texas and moved to Leesburg, VA when he was 13 years old. He has two brothers, Zachary and Mitchell.

Growing up, Taylor was a proficient Chess Player and Lego builder. When he was 12, he built is first website in HTML.

He attended The University of Virginia and received a dual degree in Classics and Math. At UVA, Taylor became a member of Alpha Tao Omega, where he met his good friend Jaryd Waegerle and his three roommates, Ian, Greg, and Aaron. Taylor was also a member of UVA’s Campus Crusade for Christ chapter, Agape. Through Agape, he met Susan Carrai, who would later become one of Emily’s good friends and introduce them to each other.

Taylor currently lives in Arlington, VA with this three roommates and works in Web Design and Development for Interactive Strategies, a Washington D.C. based design and marketing firm.

His hobbies include making dinner for Emily, website design (this one for example), and going on hikes with Emily.

Fun fact: one of the first things Taylor noticed about Emily was the Vineyard Vines whale on the back of her car.

About Emily

Emily was born in Baltimore, Maryland, but grew up in Rochester and then Albany, New York. She has two older siblings, Allison and Tim.

She was destined for Washington D.C. from childhood. As a little girl she would often pretend to be the Press Secretary, presenting eloquent speeches behind her podium/dresser to the most influential of stuffed animal reporters.

She attended Providence College in Rhode Island, where she received a B.A. in Political Marketing. After moving to DC in the Fall of 2012, she put her education to good use and currently works as an account manager at the news publication POLITICO, managing large-scale corporate marketing campaigns.

Some of Emily's hobbies are serving in the Children's ministry at her Church, Portico Arlington, being outdoors, and letting Taylor make dinner.

Emily lives in Arlington, VA with her roommate Sarah.

The Proposal

How he proposed

Before they started dating or went on their first date in Georgetown, Taylor brewed a beer and gave one to Emily. Emily thought it was the best beer she had ever had in her life. Although her friends laughed at her, she saved the beer bottle and tucked it away in her dresser. Once they started dating, the bottle sat on her dresser with a pink rose in it.

Knowing this story, Taylor took twelve beer bottles that were identical to the original and put a pink rose in each bottle. On each rose, Taylor tied a card with a reason that He wanted to spend the rest of his life with Emily, twelve reasons in all, with a bible verse corresponding to each reason.

Taylor’s best man Jaryd and his fiancé Laura set up the beer bottles on the Georgetown waterfront and then hid in the bushes to capture the proposal in action.

Needless to say, Emily said “yes”.
